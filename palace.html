The Mysore Palace was built in 1912.
It has two meeting halls inside,
known as the Durbar halls.
The palace is a massive structure which one can enter through a huge main gate.
The Palace has rooms built exclusively for special occasions.
The first room is Ambavilasa,a meeting room.
This hall is beautifully furnished in
rosewood with stained glass ceilingsand huge chandeliers.
The flooring is decorated with
semi-precious stones embedded in the mosaic flooring.
The second room is known as the Gombe Thotti,meaning a gallery of dolls. It stores a collection of beatiful dolls collected from all over the world.The next room is the Kalyana Mantapa or the royal wedding hall, decorated with peacock motifs. Most of these motifs and tiles were designed and made in England. There are several oil painting depicting the splendour of the former Mysore Maharaja and his kingdom. Another one of many rooms in the palace is the Diwan-e-Aam, where the king would meet the general population, answer their quries and help solve their problems.
